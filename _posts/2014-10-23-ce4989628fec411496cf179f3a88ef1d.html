Bill,
I live in Florissant but frequent Ferguson businesses regularly. I really appreciated your Buycott a few weeks back.

Al Sharpton is planning a 4 day rally in Ferguson Oct. 31-Nov. 3. ( not again!!!!)

I am wondering if you would organize another Buycott in the days BEFORE that, along New Florissant Road ( not West Florissant Rd.) One locally owned Italian restaurant, Vincenzo's, is right next to the Ferguson Police Station. (It is owned by Mr. Lonero who has been a wonderful entrepreneur , investing in several businesses in Ferguson.) I don't think they are getting much business these days, with the nightly protests. There are lots of small business owners and restaurants along South Florissant right near the Ferguson Police Station.

If you have time, you could take 270 and go south on New Florissant Road ( 2 miles), and see what businesses are there. Off the top of my head, I can think of a Walgreens, Shop and Save, the Whistlestop ( our favorite frozen custard stand in a quaint old train station- that got torched a few weeks ago, but is open), several Chinese restaurants, Aldi's ( has been closing early), hardware store, Andy Wurm tires, locally owned bakeries, beauty shop, Mexican restaurant, Quizno's, Faraci's pizza, etc. There are more, but those are near the police station.

The Ferguson Saturday Farmer's Market has been closed down several times.

There is a large parking lot at Aldi's where folks could park and walk 1-2 blocks to many of these businesses. These other small businesses do not have large parking lots.

Thanks for all you do, and hope to hear from you!

Lucy B.
